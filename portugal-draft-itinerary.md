# Portugal 2026 — Draft Itinerary 🇵🇹
### Fri Jul 24 – Sun Aug 2 · party of 9

**Our group:** 4 adults (39–44) · grandparent (71) · kids 12, 10, 8, 3.

**The rhythm we agreed:**
- Max **2 activities a day**.
- **Algarve:** 1–2 *full* outing days max · **at least one all beach/pool day** · most days = a half-day outing + beach/pool.
- **Sintra & Lisbon:** sightseeing days (limited only by the red-eye, the drive days, and departure).
- Dinners ~7pm, à la carte (see the dining sheet — picks slotted in below).
- Default **all 9 together**; we split or take the easy option only where a spot genuinely doesn't suit the 71-yo or 3-yo.

**Group-fit key:** 🟢 whole group · 🟡 split or take the easy version (someone sits out the hard part) · 🔵 optional / swap-in.

**Mobility assumptions to confirm:** I've assumed Grandma can do **moderate walking on uneven ground with rests**, but **not long stair climbs or steep descents**, and that the **3-yo is in a carrier** for anything uneven (stroller only on flat/paved). Tell me if that's off and I'll re-rate the 🟡 items.

## Sat Jul 25 — Arrival → Sintra *(easy half-day)*
Land **LIS 10:20**, collect 2 rental cars + WAYB seat, drive ~30 min to **Penha Longa** (Sintra). Everyone's red-eye-tired, so keep it light.

**Afternoon — pick one:**

🟢 **Cascais seafront** *(recommended)* — flat, easy seaside town: promenade, beaches, ice cream, gentle wander. Zero strain after the flight, and it's right by tonight's dinner.
*Group fit:* perfect for all ages incl. Grandma + 3-yo. Low effort, high payoff.
🔗 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cascais

🔵 **Sintra National Palace** (town center) — the twin-cone palace; the *flattest, most accessible* of the Sintra palaces if anyone's keen on a sight today.
*Group fit:* easier than Pena/Regaleira, but still town crowds + some stairs — probably more than a jet-lagged day wants.
🔗 https://www.parquesdesintra.pt

🔵 **Just rest** at Penha Longa — pool + grounds, bank energy for Pena tomorrow.

**🍷 Dinner:** **Maré – José Avillez** (Cascais, ~15 min) → backup Arola on-resort.

## Sun Jul 26 — Pena Palace → drive to Algarve
Big morning sight while everyone's fresh, then the long drive south (~2h45–3h to Pine Cliffs).

🟢 **Pena Palace** *(the must-do)* — the fairytale red-and-yellow palace on the hilltop; storybook magic for the kids, unreal views, vivid interiors.
*Group fit:* **Book the earliest timed entry** (beats crowds + heat). **Take the paid in-park shuttle** (~€3) from the gate up to the palace — skips the steep 15-min climb, which is what makes/breaks it for Grandma + the 3-yo. Inside there are cobbles + some stairs (no fully step-free route) and terraces; pace it, carrier for the 3-yo. Verdict: 🟢 whole group *with shuttle + early slot*.
🔗 https://www.parquesdesintra.pt

🔵 **Add-on if energy's high: Quinta da Regaleira (gardens)** — the famous Initiation Well (spiral staircase), grottoes, tunnels, towers. The kids (8/10/12) will *love* the well + tunnels.
*Group fit:* 🟡 **stair- and tunnel-heavy** — the well is a deep spiral descent. Realistically: adults + big kids do the well/tunnels; **Grandma + 3-yo enjoy the gardens/café**. But it's also a lot to add *before* a 3-hour drive — I'd **skip it this trip** unless we're moving fast and everyone's game.
🔗 https://www.regaleira.pt

> *Skipping: Moorish Castle (Castelo dos Mouros) — it's the most strenuous (long uneven rampart steps, fully exposed) and least essential next to Pena. Cut for this group.*

Drive south → **Pine Cliffs** check-in, settle, **beach/pool** to recover.

**🍷 Dinner:** **Maré at Pine Cliffs** (on-resort, easy first night).

## Mon Jul 27 — Algarve: Benagil caves (AM) + beach/pool (PM)
**Morning — the marquee outing.**

🟢/🟡 **Benagil Sea Caves + dolphin watching (boat)** — the iconic golden cave with the eye-of-the-sky opening, plus summer dolphin sightings. THE Algarve experience; dolphins = a kid highlight of the trip.
*Group fit — this is the one to choose carefully:*
- **Whole group 🟢:** book a **larger, stable catamaran** with easy marina boarding (dolphin-watching + cave viewed from the water). Calm summer morning seas, life jackets, ~1.5–2.5h. Workable for Grandma + 3-yo with sun cover + supervision.
- **Or split 🟡:** adults + big kids take a **small boat / kayak / SUP** *into* the cave (bumpier, more active); Grandma + 3-yo take the gentle catamaran or sit it out at the pool.
*Watch-outs:* boarding for Grandma (pick a marina, not a beach-launch), sun exposure, the 3-yo in a life jacket the whole time, possible seasickness. **Morning = calmer + cooler.**
🔗 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benagil
*Booking: easiest via Pine Cliffs concierge, or a licensed Albufeira/Armação de Pêra operator.*

**Afternoon:** beach/pool at the resort. **🍷 Dinner:** **O Pescador** (seafood) — or swap with Rohini.

*Group fit:* 🟢 effortless for everyone, no driving, beat the midday heat in the water.
🔗 https://www.pinecliffs.com/en/dining/  *(resort site)*

**🍷 Dinner:** **Rohini by Little Miss India** (the veg-anchor night).

## Wed Jul 29 — Algarve: Silves castle (AM half-day) + beach/pool (PM)
🟢 **Silves Castle + old town** (~35–40 min) — the big red-sandstone Moorish castle above a sleepy river town; walkable ramparts, cathedral, cafés. Less crowded, genuinely historic, a nice culture half-day.
*Group fit:* 🟢 mostly walkable ramparts (some uneven sections + a few steps — Grandma fine with pacing; carrier for 3-yo). Go in the **morning** before inland heat peaks.
🔗 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Castle_of_Silves

**Afternoon:** back for beach/pool. **🍷 Dinner:** **Yakuza by Olivier** (Japanese).

## Thu Jul 30 — Algarve: West-coast day (the one full outing) + Vilamoura evening
🟢/🟡 **Lagos + Ponta da Piedade** (~50 min west) — the Algarve's most spectacular cliffs: golden stacks, arches, grottoes in turquoise water. Charming, flat, walkable **Lagos old town** too.
*Group fit:*
- **Clifftop viewpoint + Lagos town:** 🟢 easy for all (flat walk to the lookout; the lighthouse area is level).
- **Down among the rocks:** 🟡 it's ~200 steep steps to the water — Grandma + 3-yo **stay up top**; the keen ones descend, or everyone takes a **small grotto boat** from Lagos marina (gentler than the stairs, and stunning).
🔗 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ponta_da_Piedade

🔵 **Optional add: Sagres / Cabo de São Vicente** (the SW tip of Europe — dramatic "end of the world" cliffs + lighthouse + fortress). **My call: skip or save it** — it's another ~45 min *past* Lagos, very exposed/windy, and turns this into a long hot driving day that the 3-yo + Grandma won't love. Gorgeous, but a lot for this group.
🔗 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cape_St._Vincent

**Evening:** **Vilamoura marina** stroll (~15 min) — flat promenade, boats, gelato, easy dinner-ish vibe; great low-effort outing for all ages. (Or just resort.)

**🍷 Dinner:** flex — **O Pescador** again, or the table favorite / Piri Piri Steak House.

Checkout, drive ~2h45–3h to **Lisbon Marriott**. Afternoon is a half-day after the drive.

🔵 **If there's energy — Castelo de São Jorge / Alfama** (else just settle in):
🟡 **Castelo de São Jorge** — hilltop Moorish castle, panoramic city + river views, peacocks, ramparts. **Take a taxi/tuk-tuk up** (Alfama's lanes are steep cobbles — rough for Grandma + 3-yo on foot). Once up top it's a flat-ish wander with huge views.
*Group fit:* 🟡 great with the taxi-up; keep the steep Alfama wandering light.
🔗 https://castelodesaojorge.pt

## Sat Aug 1 — Lisbon: the full sightseeing day
🟢 **Oceanário de Lisboa** *(morning — top pick)* — one of the world's best aquariums; the giant central tank, sea otters, penguins. Flat, indoor/AC, stroller-friendly. **The single best all-ages, beat-the-heat activity of the trip** — the 3-yo *and* the 71-yo both win.
🔗 https://www.oceanario.pt

🟢 **Belém complex** *(afternoon)* — flat, riverside, iconic:
- **Jerónimos Monastery** — vast Manueline cloisters (book ahead to skip the queue).
- **Belém Tower** — photogenic from outside; *the interior is a narrow spiral climb* — view it from the gardens, skip the climb with Grandma + 3-yo.
- **Pastéis de Belém** — the original custard tarts, warm from the oven (kid catnip).
*Group fit:* 🟢 mostly flat riverside walking; just skip the Tower's interior stairs.
🔗 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bel%C3%A9m_Tower

🟢 **Time Out Market** — lunch, not dinner: huge food hall, something for everyone incl. the vegetarian + picky kids, all under one roof.
🔗 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_Out_Market_Lisboa

**🍷 Dinner:** **Ibo** (Mozambican–Goan, riverfront) → backups Sabores de Goa / Zaafran.
